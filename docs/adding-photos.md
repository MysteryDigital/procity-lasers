# Adding project photos to the website

This guide covers how to name your photo files and add a new project through the admin panel. No technical knowledge required.

---

## Before you upload: name your files

Rename your photos on your computer or phone **before** uploading them. Good names keep the media library tidy and make it easy to find photos later.

### Rules for naming

- Use **lowercase letters, numbers, and hyphens only** — no spaces, no apostrophes, no other punctuation.
- Keep names short but descriptive — location and job type are usually enough.
- End before photos with `-before` and after photos with `-after`.

### Examples

| Job | Before file name | After file name |
|---|---|---|
| Church graffiti, Derby | `church-derby-before.jpg` | `church-derby-after.jpg` |
| Underpass, Nottingham | `underpass-nottingham-before.jpg` | `underpass-nottingham-after.jpg` |
| Factory unit, Sheffield | `factory-sheffield-before.jpg` | `factory-sheffield-after.jpg` |

If you only have one photo of a job (not a pair), just name it without any suffix:

```
warehouse-birmingham.jpg
```

## Adding a new project

Go to your admin panel at **procitylasers.com/admin** and log in.

1. Click **Projects** in the left sidebar.
2. Click **New Project** in the top right.
3. Fill in the fields (see below).
4. Click **Publish** when you're done.

---

## The project fields

### Title
The project name as it will appear on the website. Be specific — include the location and type of work.

> Grade II church, Derby — graffiti removal

### Category
Choose the one that best describes the job:

- Graffiti Removal
- Heritage Conservation
- Fire Damage
- Industrial Cleaning
- Paint Removal

### Caption *(optional)*
A short sentence describing what was done. Shown under the project on gallery pages.

> Sandstone façade. Graffiti and biological staining removed across three elevations.

## Uploading photos

There are three ways to show a project's photos. **Use only one per project.**

---

### Option A — Before and after pair (most common)

This creates the sliding comparison viewer that visitors can drag left and right.

1. Click **Choose Before Image** and upload your `-before` photo.
2. Optionally fill in **Before image caption** — a short note describing what is shown (e.g. *Spray paint across three bays of sandstone*).
3. Click **Choose After Image** and upload your `-after` photo.
4. Optionally fill in **After image caption** — a short note describing the result (e.g. *Surface fully clean, no substrate damage*).
5. Leave the **Standalone image** field empty.

> You must fill in both Before and After. One on its own will not work.

---

### Option B — Single photo

Use this if you only have one photo of a job, or if before/after isn't relevant.

1. Click **Choose Standalone Image** and upload your photo.
2. Optionally fill in **Standalone image caption** — a short note describing what is shown.
3. Leave the **Before image** and **After image** fields empty.

---

### Option C — Video

Use this for jobs where you have a YouTube or Vimeo video.

1. Paste the full video URL into the **Video URL** field (e.g. `https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=...`).
2. Optionally upload a **Video thumbnail** — a still image shown in the gallery before the video plays. If you leave this blank, YouTube thumbnails are fetched automatically. Vimeo videos will show a placeholder image if no thumbnail is uploaded.

---

## Tips for good results

- **Photograph from the same spot.** Frame your before and after shots from exactly the same position and angle — the comparison is much more striking when the two images line up.
- **Landscape orientation.** Horizontal photos (wider than they are tall) work best in the slider.
- **Good light, no filters.** Natural light without heavy phone filters shows the work most accurately. Councils and insurers are looking at the evidence, not the aesthetics.
- **Clean the lens.** Site dust on a phone lens is the most common cause of hazy photos.

---

## What happens after you publish

The website rebuilds automatically within a couple of minutes. Refresh the site and your new project will appear in the gallery under the category you chose.
